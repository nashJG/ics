    The New York State Thruway is a system of controlled-access highways spanning 569.83 miles within the U.S. state of New York. It is operated by the New York State Thruway Authority, a New York State public-benefit corporation. The 496.00-mile mainline is a toll road that extends from the New York City line at Yonkers to the Pennsylvania state line at Ripley by way of I-87 and I-90 through Albany, Syracuse, and Buffalo. According to the International Bridge, Tunnel and Turnpike Association, the Thruway is the fifth-busiest toll road in the United States.
